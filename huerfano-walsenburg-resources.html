Downtown & Main Street

downtownwalsenburg.org

Walsenburg Downtown Revitalization Committee (WDRC), a volunteer-run 501(c)(3). Site covers four standing events plus a calendar and shop:

  • Farmer's Market ("Make It Rain") — Sundays, 12 noon to 2 p.m. at Heritage Park. Fresh vegetables and plant starts, weekly through September.
  • Porchfest — WDRC's biggest event, held annually on the Saturday of Memorial Day weekend. A town-wide yard sale and block party format combined with live music and new mural unveilings, with a merch stand at Miner's Plaza benefiting WDRC directly.
  • FAR OUT Murals — the town's ongoing mural program, launched in 2020 by local artist Yul Jorgensen and WDRC founder Aubrey Lykins, originally in partnership with Meow Wolf (Denver). New murals are typically unveiled each year around Porchfest weekend.
  • Mountain Mining Days (MMD) — Walsenburg's annual heritage festival, held on the first Saturday of September. Opens with a Main Street parade, followed by a street fair with food trucks, vendors, live music, and kids' activities.
  • Calendar — a community-submission calendar (separate from the flagship events above), restricted to events within Walsenburg city limits; events elsewhere in the county are directed to Spanish Peaks Country's tourism calendar instead.

Parks & Outdoor Recreation (County & Regional)

huerfanoprd.colorado.gov

Huerfano Parks and Recreation District. Covers Cuchara Mountain Park and Walsenburg Wild Waters water park.

cucharamountainpark.org

Cuchara Mountain Park, a nearly 50-acre county-owned park at the base of the former Panadero Ski Resort, operated by the nonprofit Panadero Ski Corporation. Free to enter, with activities offered year-round:

Winter

  • Lift-served downhill skiing and snowboarding on Chair 4, with beginner and intermediate runs and a terrain park; kids ski free with an adult pass purchase.
  • Backcountry skiing and ski touring under a free "earn your turns" uphill access policy, covering all park trails plus 230 adjacent acres of USFS land.
  • Cross-country skiing on the groomed Lower Baker Creek Trail loop.
  • Snowshoeing along Lower Baker Creek and the Baker Creek Connector Trail, or up the Service Road to the Chair 3 summit.
  • Sledding and general snow play, restricted to a designated Snow Play Area (a signed waiver is required; sledding is never permitted on ski runs).

Summer

  • 18-hole disc golf course ($5 pass), with a steep front nine through the upper park and a technical back nine along the Lower Baker Creek trail.
  • Hiking on the Lower Baker Creek Trail, the Baker Creek Connector Trail, and other high-mountain routes.
  • Mountain biking, with new singletrack under active development by the Cuchara Mountain Trails Alliance via an IMBA Trail Accelerator Grant.
  • Miniature golf course.

State Trust Lands for Hunting

cpw.state.co.us/stl-finder

State Trust Land Finder tool. Covers the Hunting and Fishing Access Program (HFAP), a lease between the State Land Board and Colorado Parks and Wildlife covering roughly 973,000 acres statewide. Use this to check whether a specific parcel is open to public hunting.

slb.colorado.gov/public-access

Colorado State Land Board's public access page. Explains the trust land structure, links to the Colorado Hunting Atlas, and details HFAP lease terms from the landowner side.

Outdoor Sports

spcycling.org

Stonewall Century Ride info (August 8, 2026).

golfwalsenburg.com

Walsenburg Golf Course's own site, run by the course rather than Colorado Parks and Wildlife. A public 9-hole course sharing its entrance with Lathrop State Park, though no state park pass is required to play or eat there. Front tees play 3,110 yards (par 36), back tees 3,285 yards (par 36), women's tees 2,970 yards. Covers hours, cart rentals, the clubhouse bar and grill, mini golf, and the driving range.

playgrandote.com

Official site for Grandote Peaks Golf Club in La Veta. An 18-hole Tom Weiskopf/Jay Morrish championship course, opened 1988, playing 7,085 yards at par 72 from the longest tees, with four total tee sets. Public, with a steakhouse, lodge, RV park, events center, and golf simulator. Covers tee times, tournaments, lessons, dining, and lodging.

udisc.com/courses/cuchara-mountain-park

UDisc's course listing for the 18-hole disc golf course at Cuchara Mountain Park, established 2020. Free to play, dog-friendly, with restrooms and drinking water on site. Rated 4.3 out of 5 across community reviews; a typical round takes about 2 hours over 2.3 miles, with a moderate difficulty rating and considerable elevation change on the front nine. Also usable to log rounds via the UDisc mobile app.
